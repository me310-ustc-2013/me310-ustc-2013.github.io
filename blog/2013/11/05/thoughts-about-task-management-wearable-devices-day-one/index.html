Thoughts About Task Management Wearable Devices Day One

Nov 5th, 2013

The task management system is what I want the most. The point is the seamlessness for thinking and recording, then operating in the future. I always want such a task management system. Current stuffs on smartphone or pc reaaly does not satisfy me for a really long time.

Every time, things or thoughts just pop up while you are working or thinking. At that time, I do not want open my evernote on pc or on smartphone, I want to record what I think of immediately. Here are one scenario:

  1. Waking up this morning, I want to know whether my credit card support Hong Kong dollar. At that time my pc or smartphonne are not turned on. And for such a little task, I do not want to open my smartphone and type this task in, and of course not pc. I want to just say, find whether my credit card support Hong Kong dollar or not and it should be put my task pool for being dealt with in the future.

  2. I have written an application to record the start time of my current task(and this app was not intended for such function in the first place I was writting it ). But I have to press the button manually to record the time. And if the wearable device could record the start time and end time of the task I am working just by receiving voice control. I would be much convenient and I will be hooked by this. — For example, I first write this draft reply from 8:00 am to 8:27 am.

Right now, I am not supposed to write this idea down. But to make sure it get recorded, I still did that. And I now I forgot what I should do. So the things — stack job control system could be in work. I will not bother to even think to get myself back on track.

This raise the point of the implementation stuff mentioned by Andrew. Implementation first thinking is really my kind of thinking most time and it is really helpful for Andrew to clearly point that out. It will help me in the futher thinking. But just for this point, I do not mean implementation. I will try get my point through.

Computer is much like human. For exmaple, CPU is similar with the brain of human. And mouth, eye, nose, hand and etc are the I/O system of human. So the technology developed for computer could turn back to apply on human being. And the stack job control system is the most useful, obvious one that I could think. We are using technology in computer to enhance human’s life. So what I really want to say is not implementation, but what we could learn from the technology of computer. The case for priority queue is pretty much the same.

I hope I got my idea through. If not, feel free to ask any further questions. It is not natural to get into this mood of thinking anyway.

As for the platform, Andrew is aiming at something big than what I can think. It is really hard to build a platform like the Android or iOS. What I mean for platform is an extension or the first task management system — the next phase of work. I am not thinking of a platform for developers. I am thinking to build convenient wearble device that becomes the information center, communication center task center and whatever center of futhure people. We are making this platform. Maybe a goog analogy is an integration of several functions based on the architecture we build for the task management system. But this idea may not be better than Andrew’s platform.
